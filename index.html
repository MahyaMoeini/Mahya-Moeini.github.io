Gravity: The Force That Binds Us

Gravity is a fundamental force of nature that pulls objects toward each other. It is why apples fall from trees, why we stay on the ground, and why planets orbit stars.

Newton's Law of Gravitation: \( F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2} \)

Here:

  • \( F \): Gravitational force
  • \( G \): Gravitational constant (\(6.674 \times 10^{-11} \, \text{Nm}^2/\text{kg}^2\))
  • \( m_1 \) and \( m_2 \): Masses of two objects
  • \( r \): Distance between the objects

Gravity is not just about Earth—it governs the movement of celestial bodies, the tides, and even the structure of the universe!
